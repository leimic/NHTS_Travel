NHTS 2022 Mode Share Visualizations

These interactive visuals explore travel behavior in the U.S. using the 2022 National Household Travel Survey. They show how household vehicle access, income, urban/rural status, age, gender, and trip purpose influence transportation mode choice.

Mode Share by Vehicle Ownership

Shows how the presence of household vehicles influences transportation mode choice.

Mode Share by Vehicle Ownership and Urbanicity

Breaks down mode share across urban vs rural households by vehicle access.

Mode Share by Vehicle Ownership and Income

Examines how household income and vehicle ownership shape mode decisions.

Mode Share by Age Group

Reveals how age groups vary in their transportation preferences.

Mode Share by Gender

Compares gender differences in travel mode choices.

Mode Share by Trip Purpose

Analyzes how trip purposes (e.g., work, shopping) relate to mode choice.

Walk Share by Census Division

Highlights regional variation in walking rates across the U.S.

About and Methods

These visualizations were created using Python (Pandas, Plotly) to analyze public microdata from the 2022 National Household Travel Survey (NHTS).

The dataset includes detailed trip records linked to household and individual-level attributes such as income, age, gender, vehicle access, and location. Exploratory analysis and cross-tabulation were used to estimate the modal share of walking, biking, transit, and driving across demographic and regional groups.

Some charts reflect multivariate relationships (e.g. vehicle ownership by income and region), while others focus on descriptive insights such as trip purpose or age group behavior.

This project was built as part of a transportation behavior analysis portfolio.
